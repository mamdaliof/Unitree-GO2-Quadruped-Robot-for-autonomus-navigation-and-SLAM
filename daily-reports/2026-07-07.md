## Robotic Platform Comparison
Based on today's reviewed literature:

### Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV)
- **Advantages**:
  - Covers large areas efficiently and flies freely in 3D space.
  - Low operational and maintenance costs.
  - Can reach locations inaccessible to humans.
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Low payload capacity.(limited sensor options)
  - More challenging collision avoidance compared to ground systems.
  - Limited indoor utility due to visual obstructions and GPS dependency/unreliability.
  - Slower speeds and shorter ranges for rotary-wing designs, while fixed-wing designs cannot hover.
  - Images captured by cameras can be noisy.

### Unmanned Ground Vehicles (UGV)
- **Advantages**:
  - Simplest mechanical design.
  - High stability and payload capacity due to a low center of gravity.
  - Autonomously traverses long distances.
  - Crawler configurations perform better on slick or wet terrain.
  - Wheeled/tracked types offer smoother and more stable motion on even terrain compared to legged robots (reducing vibration-induced noise for more consistent sensor readings).
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Performs poorly on cluttered or uneven surfaces.
  - Cannot climb stairs (struggles in highly irregular environments).
  - Low height limits sensor perspective and reach in large halls.

### Legged Robots (Quadruped)
- **Advantages**:
  - High adaptability and mobility to traverse irregular, cluttered, or obstacle-dense terrains.
  - Can step over obstacles and climb stairs (enabling multi-story inspections).
  - Maintains stable contact and traction on highly irregular/hazardous surfaces where wheeled systems fail.
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Control tasks are highly computationally intensive.
  - Limited speed and lower energy efficiency compared to wheeled/tracked systems.
  - High technical complexity historically limited practical deployments outside laboratory environments.

### Wall-Climbing Robots
- **Advantages**:
  - Capable of carrying heavy payloads.
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Restricted to specific vertical walls (unsuitable for general factory floor inspection).

### Cable-Crawling Robots
- **Advantages**:
  - Capable of carrying heavy payloads 
  - Achieving accurate localization.
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Requires specialized setup and guide cables.
