## 🔬 Literature Reviews Completed Today
### 1. ANYmal in the Field: Solving Industrial Inspection of an Offshore HVDC Platform with a Quadrupedal Robot
- **Key Findings**:
  - **Mobility Advantage**: Legged locomotion (quadruped) is crucial for highly structured but challenging terrains like offshore platforms where wheeled/tracked systems fail.
  - **Early Defect Detection**: Automated mobile inspection allows more frequent patrols, enabling early identification of hot spots, leakages, gas leaks, and mechanical wear.
  - **Deployment Flow**:
    1. *Manual Mapping*: Operator remote-controls the robot to scan with LiDAR/depth camera to auto-build a 3D point cloud map.
    2. *Checkpoint Registration*: Operator marks points of interest using AR tags.
    3. *Automatic Registration*: Robot estimates 3D pose of checkpoints relative to its mapped coordinate system.
    4. *Parameter Setup*: Operator programs threshold values (e.g., maximum temperature, pressure ranges) via GUI.
- **Configuration Details**: 30 kg weight, 10 kg payload, 2–4 hours battery life, gimbal-mounted visual/thermal cameras, microphone arrays, LiDAR (100 m), and stereo cameras (7 m).

### 2. Advanced Intelligent Systems - A Review of Quadruped Robots: Structure, Control, and Autonomous Motion
- **Key Findings**:
  - **TotalEnergies Challenge**: Industrial inspection automation is driven by the fact that human operators spend ~5.5 hours per shift on routine repetitive tasks (visual checks, gauge readings, fluid sampling).
  - **Solutions Identified**:
    - *MIMROex* (Fraunhofer IPA, 2011): Teleoperated teach-and-repeat UGV with 6 DOF arm and SLAM navigation.
    - *SENSABOT* (Carnegie Mellon NREC): Teleoperated inspection robot utilizing a cog-rail stabilization system for harsh environments.
  - **Environment Constraints**: Compulsory wireless communication and battery power; must traverse dynamic 15 × 15 m multi-floor zones containing pipes, drums, valves, and pumps.
